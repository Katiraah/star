MISSION

A prosperous and competitive County in economic, social and political development offering high quality services to its people​.

VISION

To transform the livelihoods of Bomet County residents through innovative and dynamic leadership, efficient and effective mechanisms, viable partnerships while ensuring equity, integrity and community participation in a clean, secure and sustainable environment.​










Location

Bomet County is situated in the former Rift Valley Province of Kenya. Established as a county in 2013, it was carved out from the former Kericho District. The county's capital and largest town is Bomet Town.

Geography and Climate

Covering an area of approximately 2,507.50 sq kilometers, Bomet County features rolling hills and fertile plains. The altitude averages around 1,962 meters above sea level, contributing to a temperate climate with temperatures ranging from 16°C to 24°C. Rainfall is fairly evenly distributed throughout the year, with April and May being the wettest months .

Administrative and Political Structure

Bomet County is divided into five sub-counties, which also serve as electoral constituencies: Bomet Central, Bomet East, Chepalungu, Sotik, and Konoin. These are further subdivided into 25 county assembly wards and 66 locations .

Demographics

As per the 2019 census, the county has a population of 875,689 people, with a nearly equal gender distribution. The predominant ethnic group is the Kalenjin, particularly the Kipsigis sub-group, known for their rich cultural heritage and agricultural practices .

Economy

Agriculture is the backbone of the county's economy. The county is a significant producer of tea, with several multinational companies like George Williamson, Unilever, and James Finlay (Kenya) Limited operating tea factories in the area . Other agricultural activities include dairy farming and maize cultivation. Despite these economic activities, the county faces challenges such as a poverty rate of approximately 45.4% .

Education and Health

Bomet University College, a constituent college of Moi University, is located in Bomet Town. It offers various academic programs and aims to expand its schools and research centers in the coming years . In terms of healthcare, AGC Tenwek Hospital, established in 1937, is a prominent mission hospital in the county. With 361 beds, it serves as a teaching and referral hospital, offering a wide range of medical services including cardiothoracic surgery, neurosurgery, and pediatrics .

Attractions and Tourism

Bomet County boasts several natural attractions. The Mau Forest, one of Kenya's largest water catchment areas, is home to diverse wildlife and offers opportunities for hiking and birdwatching. Other attractions include Tenwek Falls, Motigo Hill, and the Nyangores River. The county's proximity to the Masai Mara National Reserve makes it a strategic location for tourists .

Notable Personalities

Linet Chepkorir, popularly known as Linet Toto, hails from Bomet County. Born in 1997, she made history by becoming the youngest woman elected to the Kenyan National Assembly as the County Woman Representative in 2022.
